Rugby Football


Rugby is a variant of football that has an unusual oval shape of the ball. The recorded place where this sport originated is in the town of Rugby in Warwickshire, United Kingdom.

There are two main codes of rugby football:

1. Rugby Union
Rugby union is played by 15 players for both teams with an oval-shaped ball. The pitch of this sport can be up to 100 meters in length and no more than 70 meters in width.

2. Rugby League
Rugby league is played by 13 players for both teams, with two allowed substitutes, and played with an oval-shaped ball. A rugby league field dimensions can be up to 100 meters in length and no more than 68 meters in width.

OTHER VARIANTS OF RUGBY FOOTBALL

1. Underwater Rugby
It is a variant of rugby football where the game is played underwater! It seems very difficult right? This sport demands abilities such as strength, speed, agility and good teamwork. Six players for both teams, plus six substitutes play it as the game itself is very exhausting. It is played in a pool with a depth of 3.5 meters up to 5 meters with goals at the bottom of the pool. The ball is filled with saltwater.

2. Beach Rugby
As the name implies, beach rugby is a rugby football variant that is played in sand. This sport do not have goalposts, only lines that are marked with tape or ropes exist. The field dimensions depend on the decision of the league, it is between 30 to 50 meters in length and 20-35 meters in width. The standard ruby ball is used and 4 to 7 players for each team play it.

3. Wheelchair rugby
This rugby football variant is for athletes with a disability. Murder ball, another name of this variant, is mostly played by twelve players for both teams, and indoors in a court dimension of 28m x 15m.
