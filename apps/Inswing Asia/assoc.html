Association Football


Association football, also known as "footy" or "footie" and "soccer", is a sport typically played by 11 players for each team, one of those is the goalkeeper. Players should kick the ball toward a goal, playing the sport by hands are strictly prohibited and will cause a foul to the player who does it, of course, with the exception for the goalkeepers who are the only one allowed for touching the ball by hands. This sport can be played indoors and outdoors, even in beach, swamp and snow!

Soccer, as well, has different variations that are developed in this modern generation, and surprisingly, there are unusual types out there. To give a hint, here are some of the developed soccer variations:

1. Beach Soccer
A soccer variant where the game is played, of course as the name implies, on sand. The players, 5 members including a goalkeeper for each team, should play in bare feet.

2. Futsal
A soccer variant that is a primarily played indoors. Teams should consist of 5 players including a goal keeper for each team.

3. Swamp Soccer
As the name implies, this soccer variant is played in swamp. Swamp soccer is originated in Finland and it is played by 6 players including a goalkeeper for each team.

4. Snow Soccer
A soccer variant, which is originated in Finland as well, that is played in snow. This game is played by 6 players including a goalkeeper for each team.
