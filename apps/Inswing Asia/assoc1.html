Equipments & Field


1. Soccer Ball

The soccer ball, depending on the competition, must have a circumference of between 27 and 28 inches and a weight between 14 and 16 ounces, and typically colored with white and black.

Soccer Ball size variants

a. Size 3
The size 3 soccer ball is the smallest size variant and is used for a soccer game with players under 8 years old. It has a circumference between 23 and 24 inches.

b. Size 4
The size 4 soccer ball is used for a game with players ranging from 8 to 12 years old. It has a circumference between 25 and 26 inches.

c. Size 5
Size 5 soccer ball is the standard size of this sport and is used for a game with players older than 12 years old. It has a circumference, as stated above, between 27 and 28 inches.

2. Players' uniform and kits

Players of the same team should wear the same color of jersey and shorts with number and name indicated at the back of their uniform. Goalkeeper's uniform should have a different appearance and color than the other players in able to easily recognize them by the referees and the players. Aside from uniform of the players, they should also wear kits that will help them during the gameplay. They should wear shin guards, gloves and other kits. Goalies and forwards should have the lightest kits, middle fielders and defensemen should have stronger and heavier shin guards to protect them from being injured and bruised, as their role in the game will receive the more stress and forces than the other players. Shin guards, or shin pads, are wearables that will protect the players' leg front or shin. Wearing other equipment that will endanger their selves are strictly discouraged.

3. Soccer Field

The common dimensions of a rectangular field in soccer are between 100 to 130 yards (90-120m) in length and between 50 to 100 yards (45-90m) in width. Internationally, the dimensions of the soccer field must have a length that ranges between 110 to 120 yards (100-110m) and must have a width that ranges from 70 to 80 yards (64-75m).

The Goal

The goal should be located at each end of the soccer field, should measure 8 yards in wide and 8 feet high, should be placed at the center of the goal line, and should be built with nets in able to determine the goals easier and to prevent the chasing of the ball.

The Goal Area

a. Goal Area
The goal area is the rectangular space that extends 6 yards out from the goal posts, and the area where free kicks are taken.

b. Penalty Area
The penalty area is the rectangular space that extends 18 yards out from the goal posts, and the area where the goalkeeper are allowed to use their hands.

c. Penalty Mark
The penalty mark is located at the center of the goal and extends 12 yards away from the goal line. It is the spot where the soccer ball is placed during the penalty kicks.

d. Penalty Arc
The penalty arc is the small arc at the top of penalty area. Only goalkeeper and kicker may enter this area during a penalty kick.
