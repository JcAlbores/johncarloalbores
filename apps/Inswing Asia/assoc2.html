Drills & Practices


Practice makes perfect. Drills, routines, exercises and practices are not only for the beginners, it is a continuous process that should be bounded a player even in his/her successful journey to the sport. Here are some drills that you can do to improve your skills in soccer:

1. By Individual

a. Speed dribble and Obstacle course dribble
This practice will aid the player in improving your dribbles by avoiding some obstacles like cones, and maintaining the control on the ball in a short time.

b. Juggling
This drill will help the player in controlling the ball by using feet, head, chest and knees. This is not the juggling that is used to do by clowns by playing and passing the ball with their hands, instead, the juggling in this sport prohibits the use of hands.

c. Off the wall
This exercise will aid the player in improving passing and kicking skills by doing this off a wall by the player himself/herself. This will practice the foot that he/she is not used to play with, because in this sport, one should learn to control both foot in order to maintain the ball possession to the team.

2. By Partner or Group

a. Monkey in the middle
This drill is the same as the popular monkey in the middle. Players should form a circle and a player, or can be more than one, should be placed in the middle. The monkey, or the player(s) in the middle, should be the one to steal the ball from the encircled players, who pass the ball back and forth to other players in the circle. This drill will aid the players to learn how to have connection between teammates, how to steal the ball from the opponent, as well as learning how to defend the ball possession from the opposing team.
