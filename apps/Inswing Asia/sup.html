Supporter


Due to the popularity of the sport, supporters are continuously increasing worldwide. These people support their chosen football team by attending into the game to watch and cheer them. However, it does not end there, they have different activities in able to cheer or to gain attention. Here are some football fansâ€™ choreography and cheering styles in supporting their desired teams:

1. Football Hooliganism
This is the act of the supporters in committing violent and unlawful behavior such as bullying, vandalism, and rioting. They are also capable of starting fights inside the stadium during a game. It is difficult to know the reason behind this crowd violence.

2. Man On!
This is the act of shouting to a player with the ball possession to warn the presence of an opposing player behind. This is done to alert the player.

3. Mexican Wave
This is the choreography of the crown, which they stand up, raise their hands, and sit down in sequence. It was first seen in 1986 FIFA World Cup in Mexico.

4. Pitch invasion
This is the act of the supporter of celebrating about the victory of their desired team, or protesting or confronting opposition fans. This is done by rushing right into the football field.

5. Poznan
This is an act of the fans of celebrating and done by turning their backs to the pitch, joining arms and jumping up and down. It was first executed by the Lech Poznan fans.

6. Tifo
It is the spectacular choreography done by supporters on the terrace of stadium.

7. Ultras
These are type of football fans that use elaborate displays during a match. Examples of displays are the use of flares, vocal support in large groups, use of banners, as well as tifo.

8. Vuvuzela
These are plastic horn-shaped horn that produces loud monotone note. It is commonly used at football matches in South Africa; this fills the stadium with its sound.
