Football Elements


1.Technique

   a. Ball Control
   It is the ability of the player to receive, control, and maintain the possession of the ball

   b. Body Control
   It is the player's ability to maintain the balance and coordination of his/her body, and the ability to have the correct form

   c. Dribbling
   It is the ability of a player to progress with the ball in different directions in order to get through his/her opponents without losing the ball possession

   d. Passing
It refers to the player's ability to send the ball to a teammate in order to have a goal or escape from the opponent's defense

2. Game Intelligence

   a. Spatial Awareness
It refers to the ability of a player to be aware of his/her surroundings, to know where the teammates and opponents are, and to see opening spaces quickly and clearly in order to make advantage for passing or dribbling.

   b. Tactical Knowledge
This refers to the player's ability to plan the strategy of the match, and knowing the perfect positions and roles of each player in the field

   c. Risk Assessment
It is the player's ability to think quickly of a decision, and to assess whatever the possible outcome of it, especially when itâ€™s a negative outcome

3. Physical Fitness

   a. Endurance
It is the ability of the player to maintain physical and mental strength in a long period of time, despite of being tired

   b. Speed
It is the ability of the player to be quick in terms of defending and opposing the enemy, and to be quick in terms of traveling from one point to another

   c. Strength and Power
There is a large difference between strength and power. Strength is the ability of an individual to exert force by using your muscles without quick movements. Power, on the other hand, is the product of combining both speed and strength. Booth strength and power are needed in order to pass or shoot the ball in long distances, maintaining the speed, and the ability to defend the ball.

4. Proper Mindset
It is the ability of a player to put strong passion and love on the sport, to accept the defeat, to make rational decisions in times of pressure, to learn and accept the feedback from coaches, and to exercise self-motivation.
